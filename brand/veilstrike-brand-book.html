Twilight Tactician

A cool, atmospheric identity for a mobile-first PvP strategy game — where the board is half-hidden, every move is calculated, and the fog itself is part of the fight.

01

Essence

Veilstrike lives at the meeting point of chess and poker: deep tactical skill, shrouded in incomplete information. The brand should feel like dusk over a battlefield you can only half-see — calm on the surface, charged underneath.

Three words

Misty — fog of war is the signature mechanic and the signature mood.  ·  Cerebral — this is a thinking game; the design respects the player's intelligence.  ·  Clean — minimalist pixel art, no clutter, gameplay reads instantly on a small screen.

Brand promise

Every match is winnable with the right read. We reward patience, deception, and nerve — not just reflexes or luck. The visual language should always make the player feel like a tactician, never a gambler.

02

Logo & wordmark

The wordmark is set in Silkscreen, a crisp pixel typeface, locked in two tones: VEIL in Mist, STRIKE in Teal Core. The split reinforces the name's two halves — concealment and decisive action.

PRIMARY LOCKUP

The mark

The app-icon mark is a single pointy-top hexagon bisected by a diagonal slash — a hex tile being "struck." The lower half sits in shadow (Abyss), the upper half catches Teal Glow light, reading as a tile half-revealed by lifting fog. It stays legible at 48×48px on a home screen.

Clear space & don'ts

Do

  • Keep clear space of one "E" height on all sides.
  • Use the mark alone for the app icon and favicon.
  • Render at integer pixel scales (1×, 2×, 3×) only.

Don't

  • Add glows, bevels, or drop shadows to the wordmark.
  • Recolor STRIKE to anything but Teal Core or Mist.
  • Anti-alias or scale the pixel logo to fractional sizes.

04

Class color system

Each of the four classes owns one hue, chosen to stay distinct from the teal UI accent and from each other — including for the most common color-vision deficiencies. Color is always paired with an icon and a shape so it never carries meaning alone.

Warrior

#C0894E · Ironwood

Warm brown. Melee, frontline pressure, durability. Icon: blade. Shape: shield.

Rogue

#E6D24A · Sulfur

Pale yellow. Stealth, burst, positioning. Icon: dagger. Shape: triangle.

Mage

#8AA6EC · Arcane

Cool periwinkle. Ranged AoE, control, risk. Icon: orb. Shape: diamond.

Priest

#CDD8E4 · Pale Halo

Soft white. Healing, buffs, denial. Icon: cross. Shape: circle.

All four pass AA (≥4.5:1) on the Slate panel background. Warrior (brown) and Rogue (yellow) are adjacent warm hues, so they're separated by lightness and the blade/dagger icons; never rely on hue alone.

06

Pixel-art guidelines

Minimalist, gameplay-first sprites that read instantly on a low-end Android screen. Consistency comes from a fixed grid, a tight per-sprite palette, and a single dark outline.

Rules

AspectStandard
Base unitSprites authored at 32×32px; tiles at 32×32. Scale by integers only (nearest-neighbor).
GridPointy-top hexagons. Map tiles align to a flat-bottom hex lattice.
Outline1px outline in Outline Black #0A111C on all unit sprites. Selective (no outline where sprite meets shadow).
Palette / sprite≤ 8 colors per sprite, drawn from the brand palette + the owning class hue.
Shading2-step max (base + 1 shadow + 1 highlight). No gradients — use ordered dithering for transitions.
Fog of warUnexplored: Abyss at 100%. Explored-but-unseen: Abyss overlay at 78% with 2px dither edge. Visible: full color.
Renderingimage-rendering: pixelated; in CSS / Phaser pixelArt:true, roundPixels:true.
07

UI tokens & components

A mobile-first system: large touch targets, low-chrome surfaces, teal reserved for the one action that matters on each screen.

Spacing & radius

Spacing scale (px): 4 8 12 16 24 32 48. Radius: 2 (chips), 4 (buttons/cards), 8 (sheets). Minimum touch target: 44×44px. Borders: 1px Line; 1.5px for focus rings in Teal Glow.

Buttons

Primary teal appears once per screen. Ghost for secondary, Surface for tertiary, Danger only for destructive/irreversible actions.

Principles

Do

  • Let the board breathe — UI floats at the edges, never over the action.
  • Use Silkscreen labels sparingly; Inter for anything a player reads under time pressure.
  • Animate state changes in 120–200ms; snappy, not flashy.

Don't

  • Stack multiple teal CTAs — it kills the hierarchy.
  • Use pure white (#FFFFFF) or pure black — always Mist and Abyss.
  • Add gloss, gradients on UI chrome, or particle spam that hides the grid.
